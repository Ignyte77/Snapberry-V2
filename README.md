# Snapberry-V2
A raspberry pi point and shoot camera which shoots 10 bit RAW and HD video without using a screen
# Updates from the last version
This updated version of the Old "Snapberry" point and shoot camera project i was working on and the biggest fix has been the autofocus timing. previously the camera was focusing on full 4K resolution because on that reason it was taking 10-20 seconds of time just to focus , but now because the camera is using a smaller area 1280x780 it can now focus on the subject in 3-4 SECONDS.

The camera also has a video function now that can shoot 1440x1080 videos in 45 fps with a bitrate of 35mb/s , this allows the camera to capture an uncompressed nostalgic dreamy-like videos in .ts format. 
NOTE- In the video mode the camera focus is fixed because the internal electromagnetic actuator inside the lens moves very slow and autofocus in video is very flimsy.
In the camera script both photo and video commands can be changed to try out different settings of the camera such as saturation,white balance,iso,exposure etc.

# Usage of the camera

The camera does not have any built in screen so the controls are very simple , click the button on the camera for just an instance to take a photo in auto mode, and hold the button for more than 2 seconds and let go to start video and click again to save the file.

# Future updates im working on

Next i will add a tft display to show the camera preview which will add the opportunity to add manual focus ,manual brightness and zooming features to take even more "artistic" photographs.
